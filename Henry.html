Childhood

Henry VIII was born on 28th June 1491 in the Palace of Placentia in Kent, England. He was born to King Henry VII and Queen Elizabeth. Being the second son, it was never expected that Henry would ascend to the throne, however he still received a high-quality education. Henry ultimately became the only option for the next monarch after his older brother, Arthur, died at age 15.

Marriages of King Henry VIII

The marriages of King Henry VIII are famed for their brutality and for the sheer number of unions that took place during his reign. In total, Henry VIII married six times. Almost every marriage was controversial, with his first even establishing Protestantism in England.

Henry VIII and the Catholic Church

When Henry wanted to divorce his first wife, Catherine of Aragon, there was strong opposition from the Pope in Rome. This is because of the special dispensation that had been given to Henry in order to marry his brother's widow. The king wanted to have the marriage dissolved and branded as illegitimate for the very law he was fighting against in the first place. However this time the Catholic Church did not oblige resulting in a split between the kingdom of England and Rome. Henry VIII established Protestantism as the primary religion of England. This marked the beginning of the Reformation in Britain. This is one of the most notable changes that took place during the Tudor period. While Henry was not overtly strict over the practice of Catholicism, his children would bring about a tumultuous relationship between religion and the crown because of this split.
