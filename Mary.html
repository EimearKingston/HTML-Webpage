Childhood

Mary I was born in February of 1516 to parents Henry VIII and Catherine of Aragon. She was the first child of King Henry VIII. Mary was baptised as a Catholic as that was still the primary religion of England at the time. Like her half-sister Elizabeth, Mary was incredibly well educated. Throughout her childhood the princess was betrothed numerous times in order to form alliances. Many were dissolved however. When Henry divorced her mother, Mary was declared illegitimate and was demoted to 'The Lady Mary' and her birthright to the crown was rescinded.

Succession

After the death of King Henry VIII, Mary I had a tumultuous climb to the throne. Her younger half-brother Edward ruled for only three years before his death, after which she should have ascended into power. However the Act of Succession that had been written in Henry's final days was ignored. This Act is what would have given Mary her right to the crown. The confusion led to a cousin, Lady Jane Grey, being appointed monarch. This reign was short-lived as the princess did not easily give up her succession rights. Grey only ruled for nine days before being executed for treason by Mary.

Reign

Mary ascended to the throne in 1553. During the course of her ruling as Queen of England, the princess became known as 'Bloody Mary'. This was because of her ruthlessness towards Protestants. Mary reinstated Catholicism as the religion of England, having spent her formative years practicing said religion as opposed to Protestantism. She authorized hundreds of burnings of heretics and was strict in her enforcement of religious law. She was concerned that if Protestants were allowed to practice and congregate in England then they may plot to overthrow her and place her half-sister Elizabeth in her stead. To ensure her control would remain steadfast the Queen searched for a husband so that an heir could be produced and displace Elizabeth from the royal line of succession. Mary married the Spanish prince Philip. She suffered through quite a few 'phantom' pregnancies. until it became apparent that no heir would be born. She died in 1558 at the age of 42, whereupon her sister Elizabeth was crowned.
